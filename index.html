As more and more young Americans are choosing not to get married before moving in with their partner or having a child, it is important to consider the ways this might impact their future in terms of financial success. Receiving an education and maintaining a full-time job can significantly improve the likelihood of staying above the poverty line, but having a child presents a financial burden that can be harder for cohabiting couples to overcome than married couples. The success sequence, which you can read about here, is an effective strategy for keeping adults out of poverty but struggles to capture the attention of younger generations who are not looking to follow a formula. The LIFE project aims to enhance this messaging by using playful data visualization to frame important life choices as game strategy.
